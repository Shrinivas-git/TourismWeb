Discover Belagavi

Belagavi – The Gateway to Karnataka Founded: 12th century (historically known as Venugrama)


Overview:
Belagavi, also known as Belgaum, is a beautiful city in North Karnataka, nestled between the Western Ghats and the Deccan Plateau.

It is known for its pleasant climate, rich heritage, and a blend of Karnataka and Maharashtra cultures. The city serves as an important commercial hub and is home to the Suvarna Vidhana Soudha, where legislative sessions of Karnataka are held.


Famous Attractions:

  • Gokak Falls – A stunning waterfall cascading from a height of 52 meters.
  • Belagavi Fort – A historical fort dating back to the 12th century, built by the Ratta dynasty.
  • Kittur Fort & Palace – The site of Rani Chennamma’s heroic battle against the British.
  • Jamboti Hills & Vajrapoha Falls – A paradise for nature lovers and trekkers.
  • Military Mahadeva Temple – An ancient temple maintained by the Indian Army.
  • Kapileshwar Temple – Known as the 'Kashi of the South.'
  • Rajhansgad Yellur Fort – A scenic hill fort offering panoramic views.

Specialties: Belagavi Kunda – A famous sweet made from milk and sugar.
Wooden Toys & Handicrafts – Traditional crafts from the region.
Silk & Cotton Textiles – A major center for high-quality textile production.
Belagavi is a must-visit destination for history lovers, nature enthusiasts, and foodies, offering a perfect blend of heritage, adventure, and local flavors.
